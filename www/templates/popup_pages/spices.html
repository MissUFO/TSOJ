Spices and Ointment for Burial

Under Jewish burial custom, the body was washed, then wrapped tightly using strips of cloth with a combination of powdered and gummy spices in the folds to hold the cloth in place and make it smell nice out of respect for the person who died

The body was wrapped from the ankles to the shoulders with a separate cloth for the head.

Aloes was probably a gum or perfume extracted from a fragrant wood. The head was often anointed and covered separately.

The women were in a hurry to bury Jesus before the Sabbath, and they probably wrapped his body and planned to come back later to add more spices and perfume.

This is another confirmation of the resurrection, because the burial cloths were wrapped tightly with what essentially was glue. It would have been like a body cast from Jesus' ankles to his shoulders. And yet on the third day he was walking around with no grave cloths.
