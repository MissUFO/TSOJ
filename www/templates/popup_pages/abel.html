The slaying of Abel, by the Italian Painter Titian

Abel

Adam and Eve's second son. His older brother Cain killed him in anger. Abel followed God's instructions for sacrificing a lamb.

Cain was a farmer and offered God produce but God didn't accept it because that wasn't what God told him to do. Cain became jealous of Abel and killed him.

Abel's sacrifice of the lamb showed that God required a blood sacrifice in order to take care of sin, and was a foreshadowing of Jesus Christ, who was the perfect Lamb of God, sacrificed as a blood offering for our sin.
